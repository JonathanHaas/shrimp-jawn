✨ Mind-Blowing Facts

The Wonderful World
of Shrimp

🫀

Heart Up Top

Shrimp have their heart located in their head — making them literally the most passionate creatures from the very top.

👀

Ancient Eyes

The mantis shrimp has 16 types of color receptors — humans only have 3. They basically see the entire rainbow plus bonus rainbows.

🥊

Power Punch

Mantis shrimp can strike with the acceleration of a bullet. Their punch creates cavitation bubbles that implode with tiny shockwaves.

🌿

Older Than Trees

Shrimp-like crustaceans have existed for over 500 million years — long before trees evolved, making them proper ancient mariners.

🔄

Swim Backwards

Shrimp propel themselves backward by rapidly curling and uncurling their tail — the original reverse gear.

🌊

Global Superstars

Shrimp are the most consumed seafood on Earth by weight. Every continent has shrimp in their waters.

Burning Shrimp Questions

Is a shrimp a bug? ⌄
Technically, shrimp are crustaceans, which makes them arthropods — the same phylum as insects. So... kind of? But sea bugs are much more delicious and considerably more charming.
Why do shrimp turn pink when cooked? ⌄
Raw shrimp have pigment molecules (astaxanthin) bound to proteins which keep them grey. Heat denatures the protein, freeing the pigment — and voilà, glorious pink. Science is delicious.
What is the "vein" in shrimp? ⌄
It's actually the shrimp's digestive tract. Deveining removes it before cooking. Yes, we're all aware of what it is. We still eat the shrimp. No regrets.
Can shrimp feel pain? ⌄
Research suggests crustaceans may have some form of nociception (pain response). It's a genuinely interesting scientific and ethical question. The shrimp themselves have not issued a statement.
Is "shrimps" a valid plural? ⌄
"Shrimp" can be both singular and plural (like "fish"). "Shrimps" is technically valid when referring to multiple species. But "shrimps" sounds funny, which is itself a reason to use it more.
